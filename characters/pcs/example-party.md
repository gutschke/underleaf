# Example party

The published Underleaf episodes include **per-PC running notes** in their DM material ("hand the lead to the Caregiver here," "the Control-seeker will try to seize this scene," etc.). Those notes are tuned for the **example party** described below.

> **This is an example, not canon. Replace it with your own table.** Where an episode's DM notes name a role ("the Caregiver," "the Outsider," "the Operator"), map it to whichever of *your* PCs best fits that **role or trait**, and ignore any note that doesn't apply to your group. The notes are written to key off *traits* (a memory-loss backstory, a knack for social control, an aggrieved streak), so even a very different party can usually find the mapping. If a role isn't represented at your table, delete or skip its notes — nothing downstream depends on the exact roster.

The five roles are drawn from [`archetypes.md`](archetypes.md). The example party is:

| Role (as the episode notes name it) | Archetype | Salient traits the DM notes key on |
|---|---|---|
| **The Investigator** (a.k.a. the Hacker) | Hacker | Tech-mastery; privacy-conscious / has reason to avoid being traced. Drives forensic/technical investigation (e.g. examining footage, pulling records). |
| **The Caregiver** | Caregiver | Medic/Insight; runs toward people in distress. The table's emotional anchor; the natural lead in scenes about a hurting NPC. |
| **The Outsider** | Outsider | Insight/Subterfuge; a backstory involving **memory or identity loss** — someone close who was forgotten, erased from records, or who "never existed" to anyone else. Pattern-noticer; high private resonance with the campaign's memory-edit phenomena. |
| **The Control-seeker** | Operator (darker key) | CHA/Influence; seeks leverage over people. May have a bond that casts a quieter PC as a **follower**. Will try to dominate social scenes and own a mystery's meaning — give them a worthy NPC to work. |
| **The Operator** | Operator | CHA/Subterfuge; **aggrieved**, argues under pressure, may be armed. Frustration needs an in-fiction target (a cover-up, an institution) and a periodic relief/win beat, or it lands on the table. |

## How to adapt this

1. **Copy the table.** Replace each row with one of your actual PCs — name plus the one or two traits the episode notes care about (do they investigate? caregive? notice patterns? dominate socially? carry grievance?).
2. **Map the roles.** Skim each episode's DM notes; wherever a role is named, you now know which of your PCs it points at.
3. **Drop what's absent.** No Control-seeker at your table? Skip the "deny them ownership" guidance. No memory-loss backstory? The first-realizer guidance keys to *any* PC whose convictions hold hardest under pressure (see the campaign's magic-discovery arc) — pick that PC instead.
4. **The DM keeps the real sheets** wherever they like (the Quire runtime, local files). This file is just the *map* the episode notes read against, kept in the repo so a fork has something concrete to edit.

## Why this exists

It keeps the published episodes portable. The running notes stay specific and useful (per-PC craft beats generic "talk to the players"), without hardwiring any one group's identities into shared campaign content — in keeping with [`README.md`](README.md)'s *"your PC is not you"* principle and the campaign's "real locale, fictional identities" charter.
