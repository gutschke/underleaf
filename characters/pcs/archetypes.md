# PC archetypes

Five suggested shapes for Underleaf player characters. **These are inspiration, not classes.** Underleaf's rules have no class system; pick whatever combination of stats, skills, and tags fits the person you want to play.

Use these archetypes as starting points, mix them, or ignore them entirely.

## A note on roles

For *Unattended Baggage* (the opening episode), the table benefits from having:

- One **technically-minded PC** who is comfortable with radios, software, or hardware (the SDR hobbyist).
- One **separately technically-minded PC** who could plausibly fix an avionics connector (the engineer / electronics hobbyist / former military electronics tech).

These two should be **different PCs** so two players carry the technical plot.

Beyond those two roles, anything goes. Subsequent episodes will not require this specific configuration; later episodes lean into social, investigative, and emotional work as much as technical.

## The five archetypes

### The Hacker

The radio-curious one. SDR hobbyist on long flights. Reads infosec blogs. Has a Hak5 logo sticker on their laptop. Plays around with embedded electronics on the weekend.

- **Likely stats**: INT +2, WIS +1, DEX +1, CON 0, STR 0, CHA +1.
- **Skill mastery**: Tech.
- **Tags**: "ham radio operator," "infosec generalist," "embedded systems hobbyist," "fluent in Python," "recovering competitive CTF player."
- **Backstory direction**: probably learned to hold an intention against pressure through *thinking against the grain* — a coder who refused to ship something they were told to ship, a researcher who held a hypothesis when everyone else moved on, a kid who held onto a project after the science fair was over.
- **Why on this flight**: tech conference; visiting an old contact; vacation with a friend.

### The Engineer

The hands-on one. Aerospace or industrial background. Has fixed real things in real factories. Knows what a torque wrench feels like.

- **Likely stats**: STR +1, DEX +1, INT +2, CON +1, WIS 0, CHA 0.
- **Skill mastery**: Craft or Tech.
- **Tags**: "former Navy electronics tech," "industrial automation engineer," "amateur drone builder," "machinist's apprentice," "EE PhD."
- **Backstory direction**: military training, or a parent who fixed cars in a garage, or a long sober period where they decided to be useful. The intention-against-pressure is usually *being right when overruled*.
- **Why on this flight**: contract work; family member; conference; consulting trip.

### The Caregiver

The empathetic one. Medical, social work, teaching, or a related field. Carries other people's emotional weight as a profession.

- **Likely stats**: WIS +2, CHA +1, INT +1, CON +1, STR 0, DEX 0.
- **Skill mastery**: Medic or Insight.
- **Tags**: "ICU nurse," "EMT," "kindergarten teacher," "trauma social worker," "youth counselor," "hospice volunteer."
- **Backstory direction**: caregivers often *have to* hold intent under pressure — keep their composure when a patient's family is screaming, advocate for a kid when the system is grinding them down. The intent-integrity is professional armor that became identity.
- **Why on this flight**: family medical emergency; conference; long-distance romance; volunteer trip.

### The Outsider

The recently-arrived or never-quite-fit one. Pattern-noticer. Sees what other people miss because they don't share other people's assumptions.

- **Likely stats**: WIS +2, INT +1, CHA 0, DEX +1, CON 0, STR +1.
- **Skill mastery**: Insight.
- **Tags**: "moved to SF six months ago," "neurodivergent," "long-distance runner," "amateur ornithologist," "raised in three countries," "competitive memory hobbyist."
- **Backstory direction**: outsiders learn to hold their own perception when everyone around them is voting otherwise. The intent-integrity is often *trusting your own eyes when the room disagrees*.
- **Why on this flight**: visiting family back home; chasing a thing they're not sure they want; running from something; running toward something.

### The Operator

The social one. Talks to anyone, makes friends quickly, can charm cover stories into existence. Could have been a journalist, a salesperson, an actor, an ex-con-artist with a heart.

- **Likely stats**: CHA +2, INT +1, WIS +1, DEX +1, STR 0, CON 0.
- **Skill mastery**: Influence.
- **Tags**: "tech journalist," "podcast host," "former campaign organizer," "amateur actor," "youth pastor (lapsed)," "professional poker player."
- **Backstory direction**: operators often grew up in environments where social fluency was survival — military family, hospitality industry parents, an immigrant childhood that required constant translation between worlds. The intent-integrity is *holding your inner shape when you're being everything to everyone*.
- **Why on this flight**: a story they want to follow; a person they want to see; a deal that's been in the works for months.

## Mixing archetypes

Real PCs are mixed. The Hacker who is also a Caregiver (a pediatric ICU nurse with a SDR hobby). The Operator who is also an Engineer (a sales engineer who could plausibly tell you about ARINC bus errors). The Outsider who is also a Hacker (a recent transplant from a smaller city who built their own SDR rig because the local scene was too small).

Pick the parts that matter for your character. Tags are free; you can have five from across categories.

## What stays consistent

No matter the archetype, every Underleaf PC:

- Answers the *intention-against-pressure* question.
- Has a real Bay Area home or recent transplant story.
- Has one personally meaningful item.
- Has a vague prior connection to one other PC.
- Has one specific reason for the Flight 887 trip.

These five elements make the campaign work mechanically — they give the DM material for coincidence-seeding, retrocausal play, and stakes. Build them out at character creation; you don't need to know yet why they matter.
