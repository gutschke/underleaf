# Accidental casting in Episode 2

This is Phase I of the magic-discovery arc (see [`../../../world/rules.md`](../../../world/rules.md) §"Discovering magic" and [`../../../design/DM-ONLY/arc.md`](../../../design/DM-ONLY/arc.md)): the players have **no cast action on their sheets**; the DM silently grants **Free or Cheap** effects to strongly-held intent, attributed in fiction to luck, and tracks them in a DM-private log. The realization beat (when a PC learns they can cast) is sessions 3–5 — **not** this episode. Episode 2 plants; it does not pay off.

## Two places it fires this session

1. **Salted luck (Scene 1).** Two small too-lucky beats across different PCs — parking spot, cancelled dread-appointment, waived fee, the last item on the shelf. Throwaway texture. Never portents.
2. **The closing cast (Scene 7).** One PC, under *authentic high-pressure intent*, gets a Cheap effect the group half-sees: the following car loses interest, too cleanly. **Default to the aggrieved/operator PC** (wants out — it's his relief beat and his moment; the hacker already got the footage-forensics spotlight). The hacker is the fallback only if the operator isn't present/engaged.

## How to grant it (the discipline)

- **Authentic want only.** The effect attaches to a want the PC genuinely, pressingly holds — not a stated tactic. (A PC who says "I cast to make the car leave" gets nothing; there's no cast on their sheet and they don't know magic exists. A PC who *desperately wants the car gone* gets the Cheap nudge.)
- **Cheap tier, loose causality.** Attention slides; a driver glances away; a light changes; a record fails to save. Never an instrumented or witnessed-impossible effect (that's expensive and would blow the deniability). The probability inversion applies: a passerby looking the wrong way is Cheap; a documented thing changing is not.
- **No roll.** Don't gate it behind a roll they can fail — that turns wonder into mechanics. Grant it silently.
- **Half-witnessed, not proven.** The group sees the *outcome* and feels the half-second wrongness; nobody sees a cause. If a player says "that was weird," agree and let it sit.

## Keep it in the coincidence register

Do **not** name it as magic; do **not** tie it to the lock; do **not** connect it to the connector. All convergence belongs to sessions 3–5, made by the players themselves. This session, it's luck — eerie luck, but luck.

**Handle the likely "wait — did *we* do that?"** A whole session of collectively-observed too-clean events makes this the natural table reaction to the closing cast. Use the *shrug* register (it's a cheap event): *"Long day. Long week."* — not the heavier "could be, could not be." Above all, **do not let the group ratify a shared "we have powers" conclusion in-session.** The realization is per-PC and *earned*, in sessions 3–5; it must not become a group decision sourced from this beat. Deflect warmly, let it sit, move on.

## What to log (required for the realization runway)

In your private `sessions/<date>/dm-reflections.md`, record:

- **Every luck/Cheap effect you granted**, to whom, and the want it attached to.
- **Which PC's wants are strongest / most authentic** — your candidate first-realizer (recommended: the Outsider with the memory-loss backstory; otherwise whoever holds their convictions hardest under pressure).
- **Whether the lock callback landed** and who, if anyone, connected it.
- **Any theory the players voiced** (especially the AI/conspiracy read) — you'll let it ripen, then dissolve it.

The sessions 3–5 realization beat harvests this log. Without it, the beat has nothing to land on.
