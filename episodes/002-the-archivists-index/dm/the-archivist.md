# The Archivist — DM script

For Scene 6, the centerpiece. Bea Ferro's sheet: [`../../../characters/npcs/bea-ferro.json`](../../../characters/npcs/bea-ferro.json). This is the hardest scene to run in the episode because of resolution-pressure; rehearse the refusal lines.

## What Bea is (and is not)

- **Is:** a retired insurance claims investigator who spent forty years on improbable-loss / "act of God" claims and noticed they cluster around certain people. She built a private index. She is the campaign's first proof the PCs' weirdness is an *old, recurring, already-catalogued* phenomenon.
- **Is not:** a caster, a network member, or a mentor. She holds no answer about the gate edit, and there is no answer to hold — the cause is permanently open.

## What the scene must deliver

1. **Scale.** Decades of the same fingerprint → the PCs are one ripple in a long pattern. (The belief-revision: *not unique → catalogued for decades.*)
2. **Being believed.** After Reggie's and Yui's denials, Bea is the first person who *doesn't* think they're crazy. That relief is the door paired with her refusal-wall.
3. **The 1987 card** — the thesis statement (below).
4. (Conditional) **The lock recategorization** — see [the-lock-callback.md](the-lock-callback.md).
5. A bridge: she is the path to Mira Chen / the network, later. Plant the *existence* of "others like this," not the network itself.

**Priority if the scene is tight.** Bea carries a lot. The **load-bearing trio** is 1 (scale / being believed), 3 (the 1987 card), and 5 (the unnamed bridge). The **lock recategorization** (4, see [the-lock-callback.md](the-lock-callback.md)) and the **Marsh seed** (below) are *droppable* — the lock resurfaces fine in sessions 3–5, and Marsh is pure Lore-Reader bonus. Cutting them protects both the clock and Bea's "one scene = one glimpse" economy; don't let her tip into an info-faucet by sheer volume of output, even when each item withholds its conclusion.

## Scene shape (the 20 minutes have a rhythm)

This is the longest scene; give it a spine so it doesn't sprawl:

1. **Arrival & warmth (~3 min).** The doorway greeting; tea; "mind the boxes." Establish her as warm and a little sad. Players relax.
2. **The method tour (~5 min).** She shows *how* she works — cards, clippings, her taxonomy of luck. This is where the lock-decoy entries live (if running them). The PCs start to grasp the *scale*.
3. **The refusal lands (~3 min).** They ask what it *means*. She declines — characterful, final (see below). The first beat of real unease: someone has already seen the pattern and chosen silence.
4. **The 1987 card (~4 min).** She pulls it unasked. The belief revises. Offer the photocopy.
5. **The bridge & exit (~5 min).** "You're not the first." She points, obliquely, at others-like-this (never the network by name). They leave with the card, the scale, and the relief of being believed.

If you must compress, keep 1 → 3 → 4 → 5 and thin the method tour.

**Greeting variants.** Scene 6's read-aloud assumes the PCs **called ahead** (*"You're the ones who called about the airport…"*). If they show up **unannounced**, use instead:

> *"You found me. Most people don't bother. …Come in, mind the boxes."*

Either way she's expecting *someone like them* eventually — that faint not-surprised quality is part of her.

## The refusal (hard invariant — rehearse)

Bea shows records and never draws the conclusion. She is warm, not evasive; her refusal is characterful and final.

> *"I keep the records. I don't explain them. You'll understand or you won't — that's the only honest way to do this."*
>
> *(if pressed)* *"They called me a crank for forty years, sweetheart. The adjusters, my own husband for a while. I stopped explaining and started just... keeping. Being believed turned out to matter more than being right out loud. So I'll show you everything. I won't tell you what it means. You wouldn't believe me anyway — you have to find it."*
>
> *(if a deep-layer player corners her, off the record)* *"I want you to find that out for yourselves, not from me."*

Bounce-backs for pressure (memorize, à la the gate script):

| Player behavior | Bea's response |
|---|---|
| "But what IS it?" | Slides a card across. *"What do you think it is?"* (genuine question; then listens) |
| "You obviously have a theory." | *"I have nine. I keep them in a box and I don't open it for company."* |
| "Is this magic? / Is it AI?" | *"People have called it both. People have called it God and fraud and bad luck. I write down what happened. I don't write down what it was."* |
| Threats / aggression | She goes quiet, warm but closed: *"I think you should have your tea and go, kiddo. The door's the records. Come back when you've looked."* |
| Charm / Influence (the Control-seeker) | She enjoys it, gives them tea and time and *more cards* — never a conclusion. Charm buys warmth, never answers. |

## Rehearsal & slip recovery

Like the gate scene, the failure mode is *you* accidentally explaining. Quick risk management:

- **Pre-session drill (2 min):** read the three refusal lines and the five bounce-backs out loud, twice. Pre-decide your answer to the one question your table's deepest player always asks: *"But what does it mean?"* → *"What do you think it means?"* (then listen — it's a real question).
- **If you slip and start explaining** (you begin to connect a dot for them): don't double down or "balance" it. Stop, have Bea catch herself — *"...but that's me talking. I said I wouldn't."* — and slide a card across instead of finishing the thought. The card carries the recovery.
- **The tell to avoid:** Bea theorizing *out loud* about causes. She *recognizes* shapes ("I have a whole drawer of those"); she never *interprets* them. Recognition is fine and chilling; interpretation breaks the invariant.

## The 1987 card (the thesis statement)

She pulls this one without being asked, and watches their faces while they read:

> *Winter 1987. A child goes into a flooded culvert outside Pacifica. A man no one can afterward identify pulls the child out and is gone before the deputies arrive. The child lives. The responding deputy's logbook has a clean forty-minute gap he could never account for. No one ever found the man.*

Same fingerprint as the gate: **an impossible save, an erased record.** Let it sit. This is the moment the belief revises: *it's older than us, it's a pattern, and the erasure is part of it.* Offer them a photocopy to take (it becomes the held image in Scene 7).

> **The vanished rescuer is a feature, not a leak.** It introduces a *human agent* the gate edit doesn't have — which feeds the "individual benefactor" misread the PCs are *supposed* to adopt now and have dissolved later (big-arc Act I: "First revelation" → "Deeper revelation"). It is a *different* event with its own unfindable agent; it names no culprit for the gate edit. Run it as intentional misdirection, not a slip.

## The Marsh seed (optional, ~1 minute)

A yellowed clipping in her files: **Dr. Reuben Marsh**, the fringe physicist with the galaxies-are-sentient hypothesis (see [`../../../design/DM-ONLY/big-arc.md`](../../../design/DM-ONLY/big-arc.md) misdirection seed bank). A Lore Reader catches it; nobody else needs to. Pays off much later.

## Per-PC / table handling

- **The Control-seeker:** working Bea socially is their lane — let them shine at it. She does not crack. This satisfies their need to dominate without letting them seize the mystery's *meaning*.
- **The Outsider (memory-loss backstory):** Bea's records (institutional fact vs. lived memory) rhyme with this PC's whole life. Let the resonance be available; let them keep it private if they keep it private. The chill can be entirely internal to that one player. **Protect it from the Control-seeker:** a follower-style bond may point them at a quiet PC (plausibly this one). If they try to narrate *for* the Outsider, bounce straight to the Outsider ("that's *you* — what do *you* notice?") and deliver the beat as a private DM↔player aside, out of their control. (See [the-lock-callback.md](the-lock-callback.md) §"Who raises it".)
- **The aggrieved/armed PC:** point any frustration at the cover-up they imagine, not at Bea. She gives them no one to be angry at — which is its own unsettling beat.
- **Everyone:** keep the *surface* task a group activity (reading the index, asking Bea questions) so no single PC monopolizes; the deeper resonances are private bonuses.

## Do not

- Let Bea explain the gate edit or name a culprit (invariant).
- Spend her as an info-faucet — she's a recurring asset and the bridge to the network. One scene = a glimpse, a refusal, a door left ajar.
- Confirm magic, AI, or conspiracy. She has heard every theory and validates none.

## What this scene sets up

Bea's existence is the bridge to **Mira Chen** (Act I→II), who — unlike Bea — will introduce the network *and* name the unknowability. A margin note, a referral, or the PCs' own follow-up leads toward Mira in a later episode. Plant the network here only as *"you are not the first"* — never named.
