# NPCs — The Archivist's Index

## Load-bearing

**Reggie Okeke** — gate agent (sheet: [`../../../characters/npcs/reggie-okeke.json`](../../../characters/npcs/reggie-okeke.json)).
- Episode-2 role: the first denial. Genuinely doesn't remember (his timeline, it didn't happen). Friendly → defensive when the questions imply he failed at his job.
- The fork: treated fairly → casual ally (and supplies/eases the footage + the Bea referral); escalated to his supervisor → closes.
- Cost texture: an unaccountable lost hour / sick-day he jokes about. Seed, not clue — no PC connects it yet.
- Do not let him half-remember.

**Yui Tanaka** — flight attendant (sheet: [`../../../characters/npcs/yui-tanaka.json`](../../../characters/npcs/yui-tanaka.json)).
- Episode-2 role: the parallel denial and the **emotional center**. Rattled, not defensive — a hole in her memory that's costing her (a write-up over an incident she can't remember; the purser application; Mei sick; lost hours).
- Kindness → recurring texture (the cost paid by people who never know they paid). Cruelty → she leaves, shaken.
- The campaign refuses to fix her. You cannot restore the memory.
- Carries the witness/edited asymmetry the realization beat needs.

**Beatrice "Bea" Ferro** — the Archivist (sheet: [`../../../characters/npcs/bea-ferro.json`](../../../characters/npcs/bea-ferro.json); full handling: [the-archivist.md](the-archivist.md)).
- Shows records, explains nothing. Not a caster, not a mentor. The bridge to the network (Mira, later). The 1987 card; the lock recategorization; the Marsh seed.

## Incidental roster (names ready — players ask at the worst moment)

Looks / sounds / wants for the people the PCs will brush against. None are load-bearing; use what helps.

- **Denise Park** — *she/her*, mid-40s, gate lead at Terminal G. Brisk, kind under the brisk. Wants her shift to end on time. *Will give up Reggie's first name to a polite face; closes to anyone throwing weight around.* (Finding Reggie — [legwork.md](legwork.md).)
- **Tom Vasquez** — *he/him*, 50s, station duty manager. By-the-book, tired of liability. *"You'll have to file that with corporate."* Appears if the PCs escalate Reggie or push the records office; a wall, not a villain.
- **Marcus Lai** — *he/him*, late 20s, airport records office. Bored, helpable, a little lonely; not part of any cover-up. Wants someone to chat with. *Social-engineerable; gets nervous and asks them to "keep this between us" if pushed.* (Getting the footage.)
- **Airline phone rep** — offshore call center, scripted, friendly, useless. A deliberate dead end that redirects the PCs to in-person routes.
- **Inez** — *she/her*, Yui's wife, graphic designer, mid-30s. Warm but protective; tired too. If the PCs come to the home: *"She's exhausted, whatever this is. If you're going to upset her, please don't."* Wants her wife okay; mildly suspicious of strangers asking questions.
- **`sunsetfog_archive` / "Improbable Bay"** — the online handle/blog that names "the lady in the Sunset who keeps the lucky-accident files." A handle, not a person, unless a PC chases it (then invent + log). Points to Bea.
- **Bea's neighbor + the cat** — an unnamed neighbor whose cat treats Bea's kitchen as a second home (per Bea's sheet). Pure warmth/texture if the PCs linger; name on the fly if they ask (e.g., "old Mr. Halloran two doors down").
- **The following car (Scene 7)** — never given a face. Almost certainly nothing; the campaign never confirms otherwise.
