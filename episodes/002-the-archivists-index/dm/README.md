## What this episode is

Episode 2 opens the gate-agent investigation from Episode 1 and **widens** the mystery rather than resolving it. It advances the gate thread for *traction, not resolution*; revises exactly one belief — *"a one-off that happened to us" → "this has happened to others for decades, and someone has been quietly cataloguing it"*; and **withholds** the magic reveal for sessions 3–5. The AI/conspiracy misread is *fed, never confirmed.*

Builds on, and assumes you have read: [`../../../design/DM-ONLY/big-arc.md`](../../../design/DM-ONLY/big-arc.md) (Act I), [`../../../design/DM-ONLY/world-truths.md`](../../../design/DM-ONLY/world-truths.md) (the cost rules / probability inversion), and [`../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-cable.md`](../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-cable.md) (what actually happened, the cost-gradient, the causation stance).

## The one craft rule for this session

**No wall without a door in the same scene.** Every dead-end (Reggie's denial, Yui's denial, the un-tamperable footage, Bea's refusal) ships paired with a human gift (an ally, a relationship, corroboration, the vertigo of scale + being believed). The table's makeup makes "investigation with no wins" the lethal failure mode; the pairing rule is the antidote.

## The table (load-bearing)

This episode's per-PC running notes are tuned for an **example party** of five archetype-roles (see [`../../../characters/pcs/example-party.md`](../../../characters/pcs/example-party.md) — editable; replace with your own table): an **Investigator/Hacker**, a **Caregiver**, an **Outsider** (memory-loss backstory), a social **Control-seeker**, and an aggrieved **Operator**. Wherever the notes name a role, **map it to whichever of your PCs fits** that role or trait, and skip what doesn't apply.

## First-realizer note

Pick now which PC realizes "I can cast" in sessions 3–5. Recommended: the **Outsider** (the memory-loss backstory) — it rhymes with the witness/edited asymmetry this episode plants. If your table has no such PC, pick whoever holds their convictions hardest under pressure. Episode 2 hands that player the trigger (Scene 3) and the cost-gradient (Scene 4); don't connect it for them.
