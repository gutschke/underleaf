# Story so far — running Episode 2 after a gap

Episode 2 opens "several weeks" after Flight 887, and your *table* may not have played in a while either. This file gets everyone back in the chair without you reconstructing it live.

## Before you run: pull these from your Episode-1 notes (2 minutes)

A short checklist. If you logged your last session (see Ep1's `08-lights-out.md` housekeeping), this is quick.

1. **Who is at the table** — fill the role map at the top of [`README.md`](README.md) (Investigator/Caregiver/Outsider/Control-seeker/Operator → your PCs).
2. **Each PC's stake** — the reason each was on Flight 887. You'll reference these in the cold open and they color who pushes the investigation.
3. **Who holds the anti-static bag** — by default {{pc:1}}. The empty bag is the campaign's first physical evidence; it's the hook of Scene 1.
4. **Did the combination-lock coincidence fire?** (Ep1 [`dm/the-lock.md`](../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-lock.md)) If yes, the lock-callback beats are live; if no, skip them. You don't need the exact digits.
5. **What luck did you grant?** Your accidental-cast log. It feeds the realization beat in sessions 3–5; keep adding to it this session.

## What the players know and believe coming in

The shared state, as the PCs understand it (not the truth):

- They were strangers on Flight 887, SFO→Taipei, who felt eerily half-familiar with each other and discovered a cluster of coincidences linking their lives.
- The plane had a fault; they traced it to a missing avionics connector and hit a dead end — then an identical connector turned up, **sealed and new, inside one PC's bag**, and none of them can explain how. The plane recovered; they made Taipei.
- They re-played the gate at SFO (the rewind) and cannot tell whether they *remembered* it or *made* it happen. You never resolved this; don't now.
- They left with the **empty anti-static bag** and a feeling that "the air had changed."
- **What they suspect:** something strange — and, increasingly, that *someone is covering something up*, maybe with AI/tech. **This is the right wrong answer.** Feed it; never confirm it.
- **What they do NOT know:** that this is magic; that they can do it; that there's a network; the word "The Quiet." None of that surfaces this episode.

## The weeks between (texture you can offer)

Everyone scattered back into ordinary Bay Area life. The group chat started — nobody's sure who made it. It goes quiet for days, then someone posts at midnight. Small lucky things have kept happening to each of them, individually, easy to shrug off (you'll seed two in Scene 1).

## Cold-open recap (read or paraphrase to re-immerse the table)

Read this at the very top of the session, before Scene 1, to wake up the table's memory:

> *"Five weeks ago, you were on a night flight to Taipei — five strangers who kept turning out to have almost-crossed paths. The plane had a fault. One of you found what was wrong and couldn't fix it. And then the part you needed was just… there, sealed and new, in a bag it had no business being in. The plane made it. You all swapped numbers in the arrivals hall without quite saying why. Since then: normal life. A group chat nobody remembers starting. And a folded-up anti-static bag somebody still hasn't thrown away. It's a Saturday. You're about to be in the same room again."*

Then go to Scene 1.

## If a player forgot, or wasn't there

- **Forgot the details:** the cold-open recap covers it; don't quiz them. Let other players fill color in-character ("remember, you were the one who—").
- **Absent last time / new player:** seat them as already part of the group (in-medias-res convention holds). Give them one of the salted-luck beats in Scene 1 to make them feel the eeriness firsthand. Their PC "was on the flight" even if the player wasn't.
- **A player wants to re-litigate the gate ("so was that real?"):** the Episode-1 answer holds — *"could be, could not be."* Don't reopen it; Episode 2 builds on the question, it doesn't answer it.
