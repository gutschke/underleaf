# Pacing — 90 to 120 minutes

## Target budget

| Scene | Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Reconvene | 12 min | Re-seat the PCs; salt luck; lock primer; decide to chase the gate. |
| 2. The Gate Agent | 15 min | Reggie's denial + the fork + the referral door. |
| 3. The Flight Attendant | 15 min | Yui — the emotional center. Not compressible below ~10. |
| 4. The Footage | 15 min | The win + the occlusion + the cost-gradient. Caper compresses; content doesn't. |
| 5. The Lead | 8 min | Connective; converge on Bea; feed the theory. |
| 6. The Index | 20 min | Centerpiece. Bea, the 1987 card, the recategorization. |
| 7. Lights Out | 10 min | The accidental cast; the held image. |
| **Total** | **~95 min** | Fits, with buffer for table chatter and rules questions. |

Buffer ~10–25 min absorbs the unanticipated.

## Scene order is flexible

Scenes 2–4 (Reggie / Yui / footage) can reorder freely; some tables chase the footage first. One deliberate option: get the footage *before* Yui, so Yui can watch herself on camera (footage-as-mirror — a stronger, sadder beat; frame as shared bewilderment, never a gotcha). Note this is a *dial*, not a free reorder: it raises the realization-seed temperature (see [the-footage.md](the-footage.md) §"Footage-as-mirror"), so choose it only for a table you're content to have theorizing hard between sessions. Otherwise run whichever order the table's choices produce.

## Not compressible

- **Scene 3 (Yui)** — the human beat is the heart; if time is tight, cut elsewhere.
- **Scene 6 (Bea)** — the belief-revision needs room. Bea answers with cards, not lectures; keep it moving without rushing the 1987 card.

## Compressible / cuttable

- **Scene 5 (The Lead)** — first to cut; route to Bea fast.
- **Scene 1** — trim the reunion if the table is impatient (keep the gate decision and, if applicable, the lock primer).
- **Scene 7** — can drop to 5 min (jump to the held image), but don't rush the half-second wrongness of the cast.

## Cadence

- Scenes 1–3 are conversational and human; voice low, unhurried.
- Scene 4 has the cold drop (the occlusion); let it land like Episode 1's bay dead-end.
- Scene 5 lifts tempo (a lead!).
- Scene 6 is warm then vertiginous.
- Scene 7 is quiet; the DM's last words measured. *"We'll see."*

## End on a beat, not a meeting

The required per-session beat is the accidental cast (Scene 7) and/or the 1987 card in hand. Never end on "...and we agreed to look into it more." The session must *give* (corroboration, a relationship, being believed, a cast that worked) even as it withholds answers.

## If you only have 75 minutes

Cut Scene 5; compress Scene 1 to the gate decision; run the footage acquisition as Reggie simply handing it over. Keep Yui and Bea whole.
