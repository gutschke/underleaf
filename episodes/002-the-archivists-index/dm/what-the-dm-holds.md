# What the DM holds this episode

The cosmology underneath is settled (see [`../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-cable.md`](../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-cable.md) and [`../../../design/DM-ONLY/world-truths.md`](../../../design/DM-ONLY/world-truths.md)). This file is the short operational version for running Episode 2.

## The truth (you know; the players don't)

The connector-in-the-bag, the five-on-one-flight, the memory holes, the lock coincidence — all of it is **self-consistency accommodation**: the world routing the cheapest path to a state it requires. **No one cast it; there is no culprit.** The caster network is real (the PCs meet it later) but casts unconsciously, so even the network can't claim this. The cause is real, reasoned, and **permanently unknowable.** This is the campaign's first taste of *load-bearing in a self-consistent world.*

You don't say any of that. You hold it so your poker face is grounded.

## The cost-gradient (the episode's quiet spine)

The Quiet spends down the anchoring ladder, cheapest first. The PCs can read it off the evidence:

| Record | What happened | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Reggie & Yui's memory | wiped wholesale | loosest, cheapest |
| {{pc:1}}'s phone timeline | small gap | personal instrument; gaps are its natural state |
| Institutional gate camera | **untouched** (pristine, occluded) | most anchored; editing it is the most expensive move, so the Quiet didn't |
| (if it fired) the bay lock | a coincidental number-match | cheap loose causality serving a structural need |

The inversion: **the harder a record is to alter, the less it was disturbed.** Surface players feel "creepy"; middle players notice "that's backwards"; deep players reverse-engineer the rubric. Keep it half-legible (Scene 4) — never voice the rule yourself.

**It's a thread, not a rubric.** The cost-gradient *is* the mechanism of magic rendered legible, so a table that assembles it has reached the realization beat's payload early — dressed as forensics. The episode's job is the *corroboration* and the *unease*, not getting the rubric assembled. If a player assembles it, **treat it exactly like a magic guess** — *"could be, could not be"* — a chill and a thread to pull in sessions 3–5, never a confirmed answer. A table that never connects the three (or four) records has lost nothing.

## What to deny / never confirm

- **That it's magic.** Even when they guess — *especially* then. *"Could be. Could not be."*
- **That it's a conspiracy / AI.** Let it feel plausible; never let an NPC confirm it. The misread is the work.
- **That the footage was tampered with.** It wasn't. A clean forensic result is the honest result. Letting them "find tampering" would validate the conspiracy and point away from the truth.
- **A culprit for the gate edit.** There isn't one. Bea names none; Reggie/Yui remember none; no benefactor appears.
- **The flattening as a pattern.** Not yet. One NPC may carry a memory-hole (Reggie's lost hour) but no eerily-agreeable NPC, and no PC connects flattening dots.

## What you may let land

- The witness/edited asymmetry: *the hole is in the edited person, not the witness.* (Primes the realization beat.)
- The scale: *this is old; someone has been keeping files.* (The episode's belief-revision.)
- The cost-gradient, if a player assembles it themselves.

## The lines you hold (reuse from Episode 1)

- *"Could be. Could not be. What does your character do?"* (any "is this magic" push)
- *"I want you to find that out for yourselves, not from me."* (off-the-record, in Bea's mouth)
- For a *cheap* coincidence (the lock): a shrug, not the poker face — *"Huh. Long flight."* Over-weighting a cheap event tips your hand.

## Required forward-link

Whatever you grant as luck, and whether the lock callback lands, **log it** for sessions 3–5 (see [the-accidental-cast.md](the-accidental-cast.md)). The realization beat needs this episode's seeds.
