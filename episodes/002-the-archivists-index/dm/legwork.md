# Legwork — finding people and getting the footage

**Governing rule (Ep1's no-hard-fail principle):** the *investigation cannot dead-end on logistics.* The PCs always eventually find the witnesses and get the footage; rolls and approaches color **how** (clean, messy, who-they-meet, what it costs), not **whether**. The real walls are the *answers* (denials, occluded footage, Bea's refusal), not the *doors*.

Note: the PCs never learned the witnesses' names in Episode 1 (the gate rewind was wordless). Finding out who they are is part of the legwork.

## Finding Reggie (the gate agent)

**Default (give them this if they don't pick a route):** Flight 887's gate (Terminal G) is public. A Saturday visit + a polite word with a coworker gets them the name and the lead: *"Gate agent that night? That'd be Reggie. He's off today — lives over in Berkeley, near North Berkeley BART."*

| Route | Roll | Hit (10+) | Partial (7–9) | Miss (≤6) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ask at the gate / a coworker | Influence | Name + that he's off-shift + a way to reach him | Name only; "I'm not giving out where he lives" | Coworker clams up; another coworker (Denise, see [npcs.md](npcs.md)) volunteers it anyway — never a dead end |
| Online (his band gigs on Mission St) | Tech/Knowledge | Name + gig listing + socials | A first name and a band, enough to triangulate | Slow, but the gig calendar still surfaces him eventually |
| Airline customer-service line | Influence | (won't name staff — scripted dead end; redirect to the gate route) | — | — |

**Safety net:** if every route stalls, a coworker simply says his name unprompted ("you mean Reggie?"). The scene is the conversation, not the manhunt.

## Finding Yui (the flight attendant)

Harder than Reggie (cabin crew are less locatable than gate staff), so this is where a **Reggie-ally pays off**.

**Default:** if Reggie warmed to them, he hands it over: *"The FA on the upper deck that night was Yui — Yui Tanaka. Works the TPE rotation. Lives down the Peninsula, Millbrae I think."* Then a coffee shop near her home, or her doorstep.

| Route | Roll | Hit (10+) | Partial (7–9) | Miss (≤6) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Reggie passes her name (if ally) | — | Automatic | — | — |
| Crew manifest (if they chased footage/records) | Tech/Subterfuge | Name + rotation + rough location | Name only | The records clerk (Marcus, see [npcs.md](npcs.md)) mentions it offhand |
| Social / online dig | Insight/Tech | Name + Millbrae | A photo and a first name | Slow but gets there |

**Safety net:** Reggie (warmed or not, if they were merely *decent* to him) will eventually cough up her first name. No manhunt.

## Getting the gate-camera footage

The win is real (they get it); the wall is what's *on* it (pristine, occluded — see [the-footage.md](the-footage.md)). **Let any path succeed.** Roll only to color how clean the acquisition is.

| Path | Roll | What it looks like | Friction |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reggie hands it over** (if ally; recommended) | — | One clean scene; relationship payoff | None — the best option |
| **Formal records/incident request** | Influence/Knowledge | Works in "a day or two" of in-fiction time; they pick it up | Slow; airline is cagey about a bag-handoff it doesn't want documented (mundane liability, NOT a cover-up) |
| **Friendly contact in airport security** | Influence | A favor gets them a viewing/copy | Owes a favor back later (DM hook) |
| **Social-engineer the records office** | Subterfuge | Talk past Marcus Lai (see [npcs.md](npcs.md)), walk out with a copy | Partial: Marcus gets nervous, wants it kept quiet |
| **The hacker pulls the DVR remotely** | Tech | Quiet, complete, deniable | Partial: leaves a faint access trace the hacker frets about (feeds the surveillance theory) |

**Outcome bands (any path):** Hit (10+) — clean copy, no strings. Partial (7–9) — they get it but with a complication (a favor owed, a nervous accomplice, a trace). Miss (≤6) — they get it anyway, but it costs (a near-miss with a supervisor — Tom Vasquez, see [npcs.md](npcs.md) — or it takes an extra in-fiction day). **Never "you don't get it."**

**If Reggie closed in Scene 2:** they simply use one of the other four paths. The footage is never gated behind his goodwill.

## Finding Bea (the Archivist) — Scene 5

See [`../scenes/05-the-lead.md`](../scenes/05-the-lead.md) for the routes; the concrete bridge:

- A name surfaces: **Bea Ferro** (via Reggie's "insurance lady" referral, the forum, or the footage's insurance/anomaly flag).
- The forum/blog trail: user **`sunsetfog_archive`** / a crank blog called **"Improbable Bay."**
- From name to address: she's quietly listed; an Influence/Investigation roll or a white-pages/public-records dig gets the Outer Sunset address. **Safety net:** the referral source can just give the address.
- They typically **call first** (Bea's greeting in Scene 6 assumes this); if they show up unannounced, use her alternate doorstep line in [the-archivist.md](the-archivist.md).
