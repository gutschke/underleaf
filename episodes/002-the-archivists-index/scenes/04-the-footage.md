# The Footage

The PCs obtain the SFO Terminal G gate-camera footage from the night of Flight 887. Full mechanics, forensic detail, and the cost-gradient are in [`../dm/the-footage.md`](../dm/the-footage.md) — read it before running this scene.

## Acquisition

Default (recommended): if Reggie became an ally in Scene 2, **he quietly supplies it** — acquisition becomes a relationship payoff and one clean scene. Otherwise run a light caper — the **full path menu** (records request / friendly contact / social-engineer Marcus the records clerk / hacker pulls the DVR), with friction and outcome bands, is in [`../dm/legwork.md`](../dm/legwork.md) §"Getting the gate-camera footage." **Don't roll to gate acquisition — they always get it; roll only to color *how* (clean vs. messy, a favor owed, a trace left).** The payload is the *content*, not the difficulty.

## The win (real)

They get the file. It is authentic, intact, full chain of custody — and it **corroborates the gate scene they played out**: there is {{pc:1}}'s bag coming off the shoulder, there is Yui near it, there is Reggie at his counter doing his job. They were not crazy. After weeks of doubt, *there they are.*

## The wall (deeper)

The one impossible instant — the connector appearing — is the one instant they cannot see. At exactly that moment the view is occluded by something utterly ordinary: Yui's torso turns across the frame, or a cart crosses, or the auto-iris blooms against the jet-bridge glare. Before: bag on shoulder. After: bag at the counter. The middle is behind a perfectly, boringly timed nothing.

> *The timestamp runs clean and continuous. You watch it three times. The bag comes off the shoulder — she's right there — and then she half-turns, her shoulder fills the frame for about a second, and when she steps clear the bag is on the counter. That's it. That's the whole thing — and the one second that would show you how is the one second you can't see.*

There is **no tampering to find.** A PC who examines the file forensically (the hacker) finds it authentic, occluded, and inconclusive — nothing to pin on anyone. For a hacker, *that* is the anomaly: a real recording of an impossible event, with no manipulation. (If the player knows real video forensics and pushes — ELA, PRNU, GOP structure, chain of custody — you have grounded answers + sources in [`../dm/footage-realism.md`](../dm/footage-realism.md).)

**Give the hacker a real win, not just dread.** Their signature skill must produce a *positive deliverable*, or this scene is "investigation with no win" for the table's best investigator. The deliverable: they can **prove the file is authentic** — intact frame index, verifying hashes, unbroken chain of custody, no re-encode. *"This is real, and I can prove it's real"* is a hard-won, found-by-skill fact only they could establish. Hand them that as a concrete accomplishment, paired with the vertigo of what it means. (See [`../dm/the-footage.md`](../dm/the-footage.md) for exactly what the examination turns up.)

## The cost-gradient (leave it half-legible)

By now three records exist and they *rank*: Reggie and Yui remember nothing (gone); {{pc:1}}'s phone has a gap for those minutes (cheap); the institutional camera is immaculate (untouched). Put all three facts plainly on the table.

**Priority — keep it a thread, not a rubric.** The scene's job is the *corroboration* + the *"backwards" unease*, NOT getting the cost-rule assembled. A table that never connects the three has lost nothing. Do **not** voice the rule yourself. If a player assembles it (*"the harder it is to fake, the less it's touched... that's backwards"*), treat it exactly like a magic guess — *"could be, could not be"* — a chill and a thread to pull in sessions 3–5, never a confirmed answer. Don't reward early assembly with confirmation.

## Optional: the maintenance-paperwork branch (lock callback — player-initiated only)

If the lock fired in Episode 1 *and* a PC digs the maintenance paperwork alongside the camera (the hacker's instinct), they turn up a handwritten line on the turnaround sheet that corroborates the hatch came open that night — and confirms nothing about how. Mundane, dead-ending, never volunteered by the DM. See [`../dm/the-lock-callback.md`](../dm/the-lock-callback.md). It only appears if a player reaches for it.

## What this scene is doing

Paying off the Episode-1 promise (they followed up; they got proof); revising the belief from "something strange happened" toward "something kept it hidden — and the way it's hidden is *backwards*"; feeding the AI/conspiracy misread (a too-clean recording reads as sophisticated tampering) without ever confirming it; and laying the cost-gradient the deep players will reverse-engineer.

## Pacing

~15 minutes. The caper compresses freely; the *content* (corroboration + occlusion + the gradient) does not. End on the held contradiction, the way Episode 1 held the disembarkation.
