# The Lead

A connective scene. Its job is to point the PCs at **Bea Ferro** and to let the table build their (wrong, intended) theory out loud.

## Routes to Bea (any one suffices — Three Clue Rule)

- **Reggie's referral** (Scene 2): the "insurance lady who took notes on index cards." A name surfaces with a little legwork.
- **The forum route**: a Glitch-in-the-Matrix-style subreddit or an old coincidence-collector's blog mentions "the lady in the Sunset who keeps the lucky-accident files." (Good for the hacker / online PCs.)
- **The insurance angle**: if they chased the footage's chain of custody or any paperwork, an "anomaly/improbable-loss" flag routes back to a retired claims investigator — her.

Pick whichever your table earned. If they earned none, Reggie's referral is the safety net.

**Concrete bridge:** the name that surfaces is **Bea Ferro**; the online trail is user **`sunsetfog_archive`** / a crank blog, **"Improbable Bay."** Name → Outer Sunset address via an Influence/Investigation roll or a public-records dig (or the referral source just gives it). Full routes + safety nets: [`../dm/legwork.md`](../dm/legwork.md) §"Finding Bea." PCs usually **call ahead** (Bea's Scene-6 greeting assumes it); for an unannounced visit she has an alternate doorstep line (see [`../dm/the-archivist.md`](../dm/the-archivist.md)).

## Let the theory build (do not confirm, do not stage it)

Let the players build their (wrong, intended) theory out loud. If they need a spark, hand one to the most suspicious PC:

> *"A camera that clean doesn't just happen. Somebody scrubbed it — or made it. The question is who."*

The theory they'll build: *someone is covering this up; maybe it's AI; the file's too clean; the witnesses were gotten to.* This is the misdirection earning its keep — the right wrong answer (see [`../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-cable.md`](../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-cable.md)). Your only job is to **let it go un-contradicted — neither confirm nor deny.** Do **not** deploy an NPC to imply a cover-up; the misread must be the players' own construction, and you simply don't knock it down. Never let anyone confirm it, and never let it attach to a *named* culprit — the energy points at "an institution / the airline / them," never a person.

**Distribute, don't bottleneck.** This theory-building isn't confined to this scene — let it breathe in the car after the footage and on the drive to Bea's. So if you cut Scene 5 for time (see [`../dm/pacing.md`](../dm/pacing.md)), the misdirection still gets aired; only the routes-to-Bea logistics are truly cuttable.

Aim the suspicious/aggrieved PCs' energy at the cover-up — the airline's liability stonewall ("they don't want a bag-handoff documented") is a productive, on-theme target — rather than at the coincidences, at an NPC-as-person, or at you.

## What this scene is doing

Converging the threads on the Archivist; letting the players invest in the dark theory (which makes its eventual dissolution land harder); keeping momentum into the centerpiece.

## Pacing

~8 minutes, and compressible to 4. If the table is dragging, route them to Bea fast and spend the time in Scene 6. This is the scene to cut if the session runs long.
