# Lights Out

The quiet close. The accidental-cast mechanics are in [`../dm/the-accidental-cast.md`](../dm/the-accidental-cast.md) — read it before running this.

## The tail (feeds the conspiracy read)

Leaving Bea's, in the fog coming back over the avenues, a car seems to be *with* them — three turns, same headlights. It feeds the surveillance theory; let the suspicious/aggrieved PCs feel watched. It is almost certainly nothing (and the campaign never confirms otherwise), but the *feeling* is real.

**If a PC drives to evade (Drive/Insight):** don't gate the outcome on the die — the tail peels off regardless (that's the cast, below). A good roll just lets them feel competent: narrate their maneuver landing *in the same beat* the other car signals and exits, so cause stays ambiguous (skill? luck? something else?). A miss: they don't shake it by driving — and then it leaves anyway.

## The first witnessed accidental cast

One PC, under authentic high-pressure intent, gets a Cheap effect — and this time **the group half-sees it.** Default this to the **aggrieved/operator PC** (he badly wants out — it's his relief beat and his moment; the hacker already had the spotlight in the footage forensics). The tail simply… loses interest. The headlights peel off at an exit. Too cleanly. The timing is a half-second too perfect.

> *You want it gone — really want it, the way you wanted things on the plane — and the car drifts right, signals, takes the off-ramp. Just like that. Somebody in the back seat says "huh." Nobody says anything else for a block.*

Played as relief in the moment. Granted silently; **logged privately** (Phase-I accidental casting). Do not name it. If a player says "that was weird," agree and let it sit.

**Handle the likely "wait — did *we* do that?"** A whole session of collectively-observed too-clean events makes this the natural table reaction. Use the *shrug* register, not the heavier "could be, could not be": *"Long day. Long week."* Do **not** let the group ratify a shared "we have powers" conclusion in-session — the realization is per-PC and earned, in sessions 3–5, not a group decision in session 2. Deflect warmly and move to the held image.

This is the arc-required "a small cast that worked" session beat — and it stays in the *coincidence* register. Do **not** tie it to the lock or to magic; that convergence belongs to sessions 3–5.

## The held image

Close on the evidence, not on a summary:

> *Two things ride home with you now. The empty anti-static bag, soft from being folded and unfolded. And the photocopy Bea let you take — a child, a culvert, a winter forty years ago, and a forty-minute hole where a man should be. You don't say much. It's like the air has changed again. You'd think you'd have answers by now. Instead you have a second piece of paper that doesn't explain the first.*

End the session here. Don't editorialize. If players ask out-of-character what's going on: smile. *"We'll see."*

## Session housekeeping

- Each player may mark up to one milestone (see [`../../../world/rules.md`](../../../world/rules.md) §Advancement).
- DM writes the public `sessions/<date>/summary.md` and a private `dm-reflections.md` — note especially **which luck/coincidences you granted** (the accidental-cast log) and **whether the lock callback landed**, for the realization beat in sessions 3–5.

## What this scene is doing

Delivering a concrete in-session beat (the cast that worked) so nobody leaves empty-handed; advancing the magic seed while holding the reveal; keeping the conspiracy misread alive (the tail); and ending on a held, ambiguous image — two pieces of evidence, a deepened not-knowing.

## Pacing

~10 minutes; compressible to 5 (jump to the held image). Don't rush the cast beat — its half-second wrongness is the whole point.
