# The Flight Attendant

**Finding her first:** harder than Reggie — this is where a Reggie-ally pays off (he passes her name). Routes + safety net in [`../dm/legwork.md`](../dm/legwork.md) §"Finding Yui." It can't dead-end.

The PCs find **Yui Tanaka** (full sheet: [`../../../characters/npcs/yui-tanaka.json`](../../../characters/npcs/yui-tanaka.json)) near her home in Millbrae (a coffee shop, or her doorstep — where her wife **Inez** may answer; see [`../dm/npcs.md`](../dm/npcs.md)). This is the episode's emotional center.

## The wall (parallel denial)

Yui has no memory of holding the bag either. Her memory is genuinely missing; she is not lying. But where Reggie was defensive, **Yui is rattled** — the conversation opens a hole she didn't know was there, and it frightens her in a way she can't name.

> *"I don't — no. I would never take a passenger's bag, that's a fireable — " (she stops) "Why are you asking me this? Did someone say I — " (the cuff tug; her tell) "I've been so tired. Mei's been sick. I've lost whole hours this month. I thought it was just..."*

## The door (the human beat)

She's the one whose memory-hole is *costing* her, on-screen:

- She's been **written up for an incident she can't remember** (a bag at a gate — possibly a *different* flight; the same impossible shape), and it's threatening the purser application she's been quietly chasing for two years.
- Mei (her three-year-old) was up sick last night; Yui has been running on no sleep and has started to distrust her own mind.

The PCs can be **kind to her or use her.** Kindness costs nothing and pays: she becomes recurring texture (the cost paid by people who never know they paid it — quiet Act II groundwork). If they're cruel or cornering, she shuts down and leaves, shaken.

**Hand the lead to the caregiver PC.** This is the scene to spotlight the caregiver/Medic (their Insight reads Yui's exhaustion and fear; their instinct is to help effectively and gently). Front-loading the caregiver's humanity here — before the louder PCs colonize the session — is a load-bearing table anchor (see [`../dm/README.md`](../dm/README.md)). Cue them: *"You're the one who clocks it — she's not stonewalling, she's scared. What do you do?"*

**The campaign refuses to fix her.** You cannot restore the memory; do not try. The restraint *is* the point.

**Rolls:** **Insight** to read her — Hit (10+): she's genuinely missing the memory, it frightens her, and it's costing her (the write-up); Partial (7–9): she's rattled but you can't tell guilt from fear; Miss (≤6): you read only exhaustion. **Influence to push** her: she shuts down regardless of the roll — *"I think you should go."* (Don't reward pushing.)

## The insight to plant (hard)

Across this scene and Reggie's, make sure the table sees the asymmetry, without naming it:

> *The edit leaves a hole in the edited person — but not in the witness. Reggie and Yui remember nothing. You remember everything.*

This is the specific evidence that primes one PC for the magic-realization beat in sessions 3–5. The natural first-realizer is the PC whose own life is a memory-erasure story; let that player feel it. Do not connect it for them.

## A possible footage-as-mirror beat (only if footage is already in hand)

If your table got the footage in Scene 4 *before* this scene, Yui watching herself on camera holding a bag she has no memory of holding is a stronger, sadder version of her rattled beat — *shared bewilderment, not a gotcha.* Frame it as two people staring at an impossible fact together, never as the PCs cornering her with proof. (Scene order is flexible; see [`../dm/pacing.md`](../dm/pacing.md).)

## What not to do

- Don't let her partially remember. Same invariant as Reggie.
- Don't let kindness *heal* the hole. It can comfort her; it cannot restore what's gone.

## What this scene is doing

Confirming the memory-edit is not unique to one PC's bad memory; landing the episode's emotional beat; planting the witness/edited asymmetry for the realization runway; and banking Yui (and her wife Inez, daughter Mei) as recurring human cost.

## Pacing

~15 minutes. This is not compressible below ~10 without losing the human beat — it's the heart of the session. If time is tight elsewhere, cut Scene 5, not this.
