# Reconvene

Several weeks have passed since Taipei. Everyone went their separate ways — and then didn't, quite. A group chat exists now. Nobody is sure who started it. It stays quiet for days and then someone posts something at midnight and three people are awake.

**Before this scene, read the cold-open recap** in [`../dm/story-so-far.md`](../dm/story-so-far.md) to re-immerse a table that last played weeks ago.

**Default opening (use this unless your table prefers another):** a café in the Mission on a Saturday late morning — two of them already at a window table, the others filtering in. The reunion is the hook; the gate is the spine.

## DM read-aloud (light touch)

> *It's a Saturday. The fog burned off an hour ago. You've been texting the others on and off for weeks — about nothing, really, the way you do with people you went through something with. Today you're actually in the same room again, or about to be. And the empty anti-static bag is still in {{pc:1}}'s pocket, because {{pc:1}} never quite threw it away.*

## Re-establish the five (one beat each)

Thirty to sixty seconds per PC. Where are they now? What's the texture of their ordinary life? Let them settle back into their characters. Don't push toward plot yet. One ready opening prompt per role, if a player needs a nudge:

- **Investigator/Hacker:** *"You've been half-watching your own network logs out of habit — still spooked from before. Phone buzzes: the chat."*
- **Caregiver:** *"You came off a hard shift; you're the one who suggested everyone actually meet up."*
- **Outsider:** *"You've been keeping a private list of the weird stuff. You haven't told anyone."*
- **Control-seeker:** *"You've been quietly keeping tabs on the others — useful people to know."*
- **Operator:** *"You're dealing with something low and grinding — a customer stiffing you, a landlord — when the chat lights up."*

## Salt the luck (pick two, keep them throwaway)

Drop two small too-lucky beats across different PCs, as ambient texture — *never* as portents. This is Phase-I accidental casting; the players attribute it to luck, and you log it privately (see [`../dm/the-accidental-cast.md`](../dm/the-accidental-cast.md)). Pick two from the menu (each is a throwaway; any one is unremarkable):

**Logistical**
- The impossible parking spot, right out front, in a neighborhood that has none.
- The bus held thirty seconds; every light green down the avenue; the last seat on a packed train.
- The one item left on the shelf — the exact part / size / sold-out thing they needed.

**Dodged dread**
- A meeting/appointment/confrontation a PC was dreading cancels itself that morning.
- A late bill waived, a fee forgiven, a mistake quietly fixed before it cost them.
- A near-miss that just… doesn't land (a fender-bender that isn't, a slip that rights itself).

**Bodily / personal**
- A test result, a worrying symptom, a bad-feeling phone call — comes back fine.
- They sleep through an alarm and it turns out not to matter; the thing they forgot wasn't needed.

**Social**
- The right person texts at the right moment; an awkward situation defuses itself.
- A stranger's small kindness, exactly when it helped.

**What to skip (too on-the-nose for session 2):** anything that points at the deep machinery — no "the AI recommended exactly the right thing," no dreams, no luck that *obviously* required someone to intervene. Keep it mundane and deniable; the unease is in the *cluster over weeks*, not any single beat.

Do not comment. If a player notices ("man, lucky"), agree and move on. **If a PC rolls Insight to "read" the luck:** it yields nothing actionable on any result — *"it's just luck; there's nothing to read into it."* Don't reward the roll with a portent.

If you can, land one of the two beats on the **aggrieved/operator PC** — something in his texture (a sketchy parking structure, a street situation that simply resolves itself). He's your closing-cast candidate (Scene 7), and an early, quiet beat keeps him from going the whole session with frustration and no payoff.

## The lock primer (optional — only if the lock fired in Episode 1)

If the combination-lock coincidence happened at your table (see [`../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-lock.md`](../../001-unattended-baggage/dm/the-lock.md)), plant one fifteen-second, un-emphasized beat for the PC who held or used that combination. Say it once, doing another job, and move straight on — do **not** pause, repeat the number with emphasis, editorialize, or look at the player:

> *Sometime this week, the PC who held (or used) that combination on the plane spins their gym-locker dial — the same four digits, by habit — and heads in.*

That's the whole beat. Keep it **mundane and inert** — no eerie hesitation, no "the number feels like it belongs to something else." Its only job is to keep the digits *warm* in the table's memory; Bea's index supplies all the connective charge later (see [`../dm/the-lock-callback.md`](../dm/the-lock-callback.md)). If the lock did not fire in Episode 1, skip this entirely.

## The decision to chase the gate

Let it crystallize from a PC, not from you. The natural driver is **{{pc:1}}** (it happened to them; they kept the bag) or the most suspicious PC at your table. Bait gently if needed — a ready, quotable line:

> *{{pc:1}} sets the folded anti-static bag on the table. "Okay. I can't be the only one who still thinks about this. I want to know who held my bag at that gate."*

Other nudges if that doesn't catch: someone tried to look the flight attendant up and got nowhere, and it nags; or the group chat has a thread nobody finished — *what actually happened down there?*

By the end of the scene the table has decided, in their own words, to follow up on the gate. **Hand off to Scene 2 on the question:** *"So — how do we even find the gate agent?"* (Procedure: [`../dm/legwork.md`](../dm/legwork.md).)

That decision is the scene's job.

## What this scene is doing

Re-seating the players in their characters; re-anchoring the central mystery (the bag); seeding the personal-luck pattern the realization beat will later harvest; and — if applicable — keeping the lock digits warm. It also makes one belief-revision possible later by establishing *normal life* as the baseline the weirdness will disturb.

## Pacing

Keep it to ~12 minutes. If the table is riffing happily on their reunion, let it breathe a little; if they're impatient, get to the gate decision fast. End on momentum, not on chatter.
