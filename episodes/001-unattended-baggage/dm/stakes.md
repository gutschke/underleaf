# Stakes — Scene 4 menu

The eight-hour delay (escalating to twelve, then open-ended) only becomes urgent because each PC has a stake. Pick one per PC. At least *one* PC's stake should have crystallized in the last 72 hours — the visibly-recent travel decision is what makes the convergence feel orchestrated.

Give each PC's stake a *moment* in Scene 4. Don't pile them on; round-robin through the table and let each player describe the cost of the delay for their character.

## The family-medical stakes

- **The cousin's premature baby.** Cousin's partner went into labor four weeks early. PC was called three days ago — could they fly to Taipei for two weeks to help? They booked the flight 48 hours ago. The cousin is alone in a Taipei NICU; landing 14 hours late means missing the next discharge meeting.
- **The parent in hospice.** Parent is being transferred to a hospice facility tomorrow morning local time. PC needs to be there for the move-in — they have something specific they have wanted to say for months, and the parent's cognitive window is small.
- **The grandparent's last birthday.** PC's grandparent is turning 92 tomorrow. Family knows it might be the last one. PC is the only sibling making it from California.
- **The sibling's surgery.** Routine but anesthesia-required. PC is the only family within flying distance. Surgery is at 09:00 local.

## The career stakes

- **The visa appointment.** PC is renewing a work visa. The interview was rescheduled eight months out from the original date. Non-rebookable for at least six more months. PC's employment depends on it.
- **The conference talk.** PC is the opening keynote at a small conference Wednesday morning. Cannot be remote. The talk took six months to prepare.
- **The investor meeting.** PC's small company is meeting a single specific investor Wednesday afternoon. The investor is in Taipei for 36 hours and only this window works.
- **The handoff.** PC is replacing a colleague who is being recalled to family. The handoff has to happen in-person, in Taipei, Wednesday. The colleague's flight back to Europe is non-rebookable.

## The personal-event stakes

- **The wedding.** PC is the best person / officiant / maid of honor for a friend's wedding in 48 hours. Rehearsal dinner is tomorrow night. Cannot be replaced.
- **The custody handoff.** PC is in Taipei to take physical custody of their child for two weeks; the other parent's flight back to their work commitments is non-rebookable.
- **The funeral.** PC is flying to a funeral. The service is fixed by the family at 10:00 local Wednesday.
- **The opera premiere.** PC is the lighting designer for a small opera company's opening night. Tech rehearsal is Wednesday morning.

## The personal-state stakes

- **The forty-hour wake.** PC has been functioning on extreme sleep deprivation to get on this flight. Eight more hours of airport limbo plus another flight is dangerous. They are at the edge.
- **The medication.** PC is dosing a medication that requires precise timing; an extra eight-hour layover means a missed dose at a critical point in a titration schedule.
- **The pet.** PC has someone caring for their pet, who needs medication every twelve hours. The caretaker can stretch coverage by a few hours but not by 8-12. PC is wracked with guilt about it.

## How to pick

For five PCs, choose stakes from *different* categories — don't have three career stakes and two family stakes. The diversity of urgencies is the texture.

Try to give the *forty-hour wake* or *the medication* to a PC who would benefit from being slightly off-balance during Scene 7. A PC whose player tends toward control may benefit from a stake that loosens their composure for the rewind scene.

## What you do not say

Do not tell the players that their stake is going to be *resolved* in episode 1. The connector reveal allows the plane to continue on schedule — the stakes are resolved with it — but the players should feel the weight throughout. Resolving the plot resolves the stakes; the resolution is the relief, not a setup.

## What stakes also do

The stakes scene is also when the DM seeds episode-2 follow-up energy. PCs who experience the delay scare with their stakes hanging over them will be the most invested in the gate-agent investigation that opens episode 2 — they want to know what *almost* cost them their thing. Their motivation for the investigation comes for free.
