# Coincidences — Scene 2 menu

The coincidence pattern is *the* take-away of episode 1. Pick three or four from this list during prep, mapped to specific PCs. Drop them into Scene 2's conversation as casual reveals, not as DM-narrated portents.

The eerie part is the *cluster*. Any one of these is unremarkable. Three of them between five strangers on a 14-hour flight is when the table starts to feel watched.

## Shared geography

- **Outer Sunset block** — two PCs lived within four blocks of each other as children, possibly at the same time, possibly a few years apart.
- **Same elementary school** — two PCs attended the same school in different years. They had the same kindergarten teacher.
- **Same Berkeley dorm** — two PCs lived in the same Cal dorm in different semesters.
- **Bernal Heights coffee shop** — three PCs are regulars at the same independent café (Pinhole Coffee, the Sunrise on Cortland, or invent one in the user's neighborhood).
- **Foster City Saturday market** — two PCs go to the same farmers' market most Saturdays and have probably passed each other.

## Shared social graph

- **Mutual friend** — two PCs share a friend they both know from different contexts (a partner of one is a former coworker of the other; cousins of cousins; people who showed up in both PCs' Instagram explore pages for years).
- **Same wedding** — two PCs attended a friend-of-a-friend's wedding in Mendocino in 2022 and don't recall meeting.
- **Same dating app match** — two PCs swiped on each other years ago. Nothing came of it. Neither remembered until just now.
- **Same therapist** — two PCs see (or saw) the same therapist. Therapist's name comes up; both pause.
- **Same Burning Man camp** — two PCs were nominally in the same theme camp in 2019. Did not meet.

## Shared events

- **Folsom-Street power outage** — three PCs were in SF for the August 2022 outage and have a story about it. Two of those stories happen at the same intersection.
- **2018 Outside Lands** — two PCs were at the same set (Florence + the Machine, the Janelle Monáe sunset slot, or whatever your group recognizes).
- **2024 King Tides event** — two PCs were at the Embarcadero the same morning, both photographing the water.
- **2025 SF Marathon** — two PCs ran it. One finished; one didn't. Their bib numbers are within 50 of each other.

## Shared near-misses

- **The Powell BART incident** — two PCs were on the same delayed train; one got off at Montgomery, the other rode all the way.
- **The Salesforce Park bee swarm** — three PCs were in the park within twenty minutes of each other on the day of the bees.
- **The Hayes Valley building fire of 2023** — two PCs were evacuated from buildings on the same block.

## Shared digital footprint

- **Reply to a Hacker News thread** — two PCs replied to the same niche HN post in 2021 and remember the topic.
- **Same Reddit AMA** — three PCs asked questions in the same AMA (some indie game dev, an author, a former intelligence officer).
- **Same Mastodon instance** — two PCs are on the same small Mastodon server and follow some of the same people without ever talking.

## Tone notes

- The PC describing the coincidence should *not* be presenting it as evidence. They should sound mildly bemused: *"Oh weird, you went to —"*. The DM resists the urge to telegraph weight.
- Skip any coincidence that doesn't fit the PCs naturally. Forced coincidences feel forced.
- The DM may invent new ones that are specific to the players' actual lives — keep them aligned with the [possible-future-not-prophecy](../../../design/DM-ONLY/principles.md) principle: real locales, fictional identities.

## What to skip in this episode

Do *not* surface coincidences that point at the deep cosmology yet:

- "We both took the same Coursera ML class" — pulls toward the AI throughline; save for episode 3+.
- "We both had a parent who worked at SRI" — pulls toward the early-AI-history thread; save for mid-campaign.
- "We both had grandparents who claimed to have seen something they couldn't explain" — pulls toward magic too directly; never use in episode 1.

The episode-1 coincidences are *innocent*. The unease comes from the cluster, not from the content.
