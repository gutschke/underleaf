# Cover stories — Scene 5 menu

Scene 5 (the snoop) is sneak-comedy. The PCs need to be in the forward galley unobserved for two or three minutes. Let the players propose; weave from this menu when they stall.

## PC-initiated covers

These work because they're things passengers actually do.

- **"Looking for my dropped earplug / contact lens"** — kneel by the lavatory floor, fumble, apologize. Buys time but invites help.
- **"Severe motion sickness"** — request a quiet corner near the galley; ask if there's a less-turbulent spot. Buys sympathy and a brief alone window.
- **"My wife / partner needs ice water for medication"** — galley access for the asking. Buys the galley itself for thirty seconds.
- **"Tying my shoe / fixing my pants"** — kneel, take longer than reasonable. Cheap but works.
- **"I left a book in the seat-back pocket two rows back"** — lets PC wander.
- **"Trying to find a plug for my phone — outlet's not working at my seat"** — gives an excuse to be near walls and panels.
- **"Where is the second bathroom? The forward one was occupied for ages"** — buys passage through the galley.
- **"I think I left my passport at the gate, can I check the seat I was assigned originally?"** — lets PC near forward seats, alarms the attendant slightly which is fine.

## Distraction NPCs (use to peel attendants away)

The DM has these to deploy as needed. Each is the kind of small interruption that a real flight attendant has to handle.

- **The toddler tantrum** — a child two rows back has begun the unmissable tantrum. The parent is overwhelmed. An attendant pulls toward it.
- **The medical-ish concern** — a passenger is asking for assistance with what may or may not be a real medical issue (an "anxiety attack" that's just bad turbulence; a "headache that's getting worse"; "do you have aspirin"). An attendant is pulled away for at least 90 seconds.
- **The drunk** — a passenger has been drinking heavily; demands another bottle of wine; getting loud. An attendant has to gently deflect.
- **The temperature complaint** — *"I am freezing / cooking, can you adjust the air?"* — sends an attendant to fiddle with a control panel out of sight.
- **The fellow attendant rotation** — the crew is rotating duties. One attendant disappears to the crew rest area for ten minutes.
- **The seatbelt sign** — the captain illuminates the seatbelt sign for turbulence. Attendants stop service and strap in. (This is *bad* for the snoop; if PCs are mid-snoop, they have to commit or abort.)
- **The lavatory clog** — one of the lavatories has a problem. An attendant has to investigate.

## Failed cover — what happens

If the PCs are caught, the DM resists the temptation to escalate. The narrative wants to keep flowing.

- An attendant says *"Sir / ma'am, you can't be back here. Please return to your seat."* Firm but not alarmed.
- If the PC backs off with a quick apology, the attendant moves on — they have other things to do.
- Repeat offences attract attention; the attendant may radio a colleague. After two strikes, a senior crew member may approach.
- *Do not* call the marshals. Do not escalate to TSA. This is not a heist; the consequences are social embarrassment, not arrest.

## Insight-check cues

If a PC asks to scope the situation, the DM rolls 2d6 + WIS in their head and narrates accordingly. Sample answers:

- **10+**: "The attendant nearest the galley is preoccupied with the toddler. The other one stepped back to the crew rest area five minutes ago. There's a passenger across the aisle watching the cabin but not actively suspicious."
- **7-9**: "The attendant is busy but glances up between things. You'd have about ninety seconds."
- **6 or less**: "It's hard to tell. There's movement near the galley but you can't read what they're doing."

## The bored passenger

The persistent obstacle. A passenger across the aisle who has been awake for the whole flight and is bored. They see things. The DM uses them as a wildcard.

- They might ask a PC what they're doing.
- They might comment in passing to an attendant.
- They might assume the PCs are filming a YouTube prank.
- They might fall asleep at the convenient moment.

This NPC needs no name. The DM uses them when the table needs a reminder that observation is a real cost.

## Notes

- The DM should not have the PCs *fail* their goal in Scene 5. The whole episode hinges on them reaching the avionics bay. If a cover comprehensively breaks down, the DM gives them a second window five minutes later — a different attendant, a different distraction.
- The fun of Scene 5 is the table inventing the covers. The DM's role is to nod, to throw obstacles, and to keep things moving.
