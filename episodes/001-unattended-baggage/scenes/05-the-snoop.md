# The Snoop

The PCs need to find a way to the aircraft's avionics. They start by mapping what they can access without raising eyebrows.

## The geography (DM reference)

The 747 has its **Electrical and Electronics (E&E) bay** in the forward lower fuselage, directly under the cockpit. The cockpit itself is on the upper deck. The PCs are also on the upper deck. To reach the E&E bay from inside the aircraft, they need to:

1. Descend the small stairway from the upper deck to the main deck (this is unremarkable; passengers do it to use main-deck lavatories or to stretch their legs).
2. Make their way to the **forward galley** at the very nose of the main-deck cabin.
3. Find the **floor access panel** that gives interior service access to the E&E bay.

The floor panel is real-world plausible (see [`dm/avionics-realism.md`](../dm/avionics-realism.md)). It sits beneath a removable section of the galley flooring, with a recessed handle and a flush latch. Crew-only. Not advertised to passengers. A short ladder of four or five rungs descends.

## The obstacles

Two flight attendants are working the forward main-deck galley. One is doing the second-half-of-flight meal service (light snacks, water). The other is in the crew rest area immediately aft of the galley — visible through a curtain, occasionally moving around.

A passenger across the aisle is awake and bored and has been watching people walk past.

## The plan

The PCs need to invent a reason to be in the forward galley for the two-to-three minutes it takes to open the panel and descend. The DM has a menu of cover stories prepared in [`dm/cover-stories.md`](../dm/cover-stories.md). Let the players propose. The DM weaves.

Typical successful patterns:
- One PC distracts the attendant (asks for something complicated — herbal tea, the specific brand of cookie, allergy questions).
- A second PC creates a brief plausible reason to be in the galley (lost something — earplugs, a contact lens — and needs to look on the floor).
- A third PC, the tech-comfortable one (not {{pc:3}}, who is busy with their laptop monitoring), opens the panel and descends.
- A fourth PC stays at the top of the stairway to spot for crew movement.
- The fifth PC handles whatever the table makes up.

The DM rolls Insight or asks for an Influence check at one or two pivot points. The goal is play, not gating; on a partial success, the cover wobbles and someone has to think on their feet.

## DM cues

If the table stalls, drop one of these:

- An attendant says, "Anything I can help you with, sir/ma'am?" with friendly suspicion.
- The bored passenger leans over: *"Hey, are you guys filming something? Tiktok? Vlog?"*
- The PA chimes for an unrelated reason (turbulence ahead).

Don't let the obstacle become catastrophic. The point is sneak-comedy, not a heist. If the PCs are creative, they get the access. The interesting work is in the bay, not in the galley.

## Reaching the panel

When a PC opens the floor panel:

> *The handle clicks once and the panel lifts. Cold air rises through the gap — twenty degrees colder than the cabin, faintly metallic. You can see a steel rung ladder descending into amber half-light.*
>
> *Looking down: a narrow space, maybe seven feet deep. Walls lined with rectangular black boxes — black to the eye but each carrying a small green or amber LED. The smell of plastic and ozone. The hum of the bay is different from the cabin hum: lower, more pulsing.*

End the scene as the PC starts to descend.

## What this scene is doing

Adding tension and table fun. Giving the social-skilled PCs and the cover-story-improvisers their moment. Demonstrating that the world has texture and obstacles.

## Pacing note

If the table is enjoying the sneak, give it more time. If the players are flagging, compress. Don't let the snoop become the longest scene of the episode — the bay (Scene 6) and the gate (Scene 7) are the substance.
