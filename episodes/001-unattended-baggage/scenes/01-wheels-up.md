# Wheels Up

The cabin lights dimmed forty minutes ago, just after the meal service. Outside the windows there is nothing — the Pacific is a black sheet under a black sky, and the only light is the rectangle of each seatback screen showing a slow plane icon crawling over a featureless ocean. The hum of the engines is steady. Somewhere two rows back, a baby was crying earlier; it has been quiet for ten minutes now. A flight attendant moves softly through the aisle with a tray of water.

You are already seated.

## DM read-aloud (light touch)

You may read or paraphrase. Keep your voice low to match the dim cabin.

> *Hour six of fourteen. You don't know the names of the people around you yet. You've registered them — the way you register anyone in a small shared space over a long flight — but no one has introduced themselves. The upper-deck business cabin holds maybe twenty seats and most of them are empty; this is a quiet Tuesday-night crossing.*

## Vignette prompts (one per PC)

Place each PC. Ask each player, in turn, what their character is doing right now. Keep it short — thirty to ninety seconds per PC. Do not push toward conflict; this scene is texture.

- **{{pc:1}}** (probable bag-owner, see [`dm/the-gate.md`](../dm/the-gate.md)): in a window seat. Has been trying to sleep; not succeeding. Their carry-on is in the overhead bin two rows ahead of them, because their assigned bin was full at boarding.
- **{{pc:2}}**: in the aisle seat opposite, reading something on a phone or paperback. Headphones on, ambient noise. Not paying attention to the cabin specifically.
- **{{pc:3}}**: a row behind, with a laptop open on the seatback tray. Screen brightness low. Headphones in. Something nerdy on the screen — terminal windows, frequency waterfall plots, code. (Not yet doing anything that would interest the others.)
- **{{pc:4}}**: across the cabin. Has been crying quietly for some of the flight. Tissue, half-empty water bottle, the kind of careful composure that suggests the crying was about something specific.
- **{{pc:5}}**: closest to the galley, where the flight attendants come and go. Awake. Reading the back of the meal-service card with too much attention. Or staring at the window. Their tray table is up.

## How introductions happen

Don't force them. Let the natural rhythms of a small cabin produce them:

- Someone reaches for a charger and the only available outlet is at someone else's seat.
- An attendant offers water; two PCs accept and end up speaking briefly across the aisle.
- A laptop screen catches a neighbor's eye in the dim cabin.
- A PC sneezes and another offers a tissue.

Let the players invent the spark. If no spark arrives in five minutes, the DM can have a flight attendant ask a PC if they'd like to switch to an empty row, opening a conversation about why someone might want to.

By the end of this scene, three or four of the five PCs should have at least exchanged a name. Two of them may still be near-strangers across the cabin. That's fine.

## What this scene is doing

Establishing presence, atmosphere, and the slow-burn opening rhythm. No plot yet. The eeriness arrives in the next scene; this scene is the table getting comfortable.

Resist the temptation to seed mysteries here. Save them.
