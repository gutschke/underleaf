# Spells

> **Player note:** the very existence of catalogued spells in Underleaf is a campaign discovery. If you are a player, this folder will remain empty or sparsely populated for a long time during play. Browsing it ahead of that point may not spoil much directly, but it telegraphs material the campaign would rather reveal at its own pace.

In Underleaf, spells are emergent records of patterns that have reliably worked — not a known catalog a caster studies. When (and if) PCs come to understand that they can cast, individual entries land here as documentation of what each PC has found to work for them.

The Quire schema for a spell record is a hint, not a contract. Each entry typically has: an `intent` (what the pattern aims to do), an `algorithm` (the shape of the asking — words, gestures, code), a `domain` (which conceptual territory it operates in), and a `history` (when this was first observed, who has used it).

For the cosmology behind why any of this works, see the DM-only design notes.
